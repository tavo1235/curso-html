Texas Hold'em is the most popular version of poker, known for its "easy to learn, hard to master" reputation.Here is a breakdown of how the game works, from the basic setup to winning the pot.

The Core Concept

Each player is dealt two private cards (hole cards). Five community cards are then dealt face-up in the middle of the table. Your goal is to make the best five-card hand possible using any combination of your two hole cards and the five community cards.

Hand Rankings (Highest to Lowest)

  1. Royal Flush: A, K, Q, J, 10 all of the same suit.
  2. Straight Flush: Five cards in sequence, all of the same suit.
  3. Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank.
  4. Full House: Three of a kind plus a pair.
  5. Flush: Any five cards of the same suit (not in sequence).
  6. Straight: Five cards in sequence (different suits).
  7. Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank.
  8. Two Pair: Two different pairs.
  9. One Pair: Two cards of the same rank.
  10. High Card: When you have none of the above, your highest card counts.

Pro Tips for Beginners

  • Don't play every hand: Most pros fold about 70-80% of their starting hands. If your two cards aren't strong (like a high pair or big cards like Ace-King), it’s often better to fold early.
  • Position is power: Being the last person to act in a betting round is a huge advantage because you get to see what everyone else did before you make your move.
  • Watch the "Blinds": Before cards are dealt, the two players to the left of the dealer must put in forced bets (Small Blind and Big Blind) to ensure there is always money to play for.
  • The Showdown: If two or more players remain after the final betting round, they show their cards. The best hand wins the entire pot. If everyone folds but one player, that player wins without having to show their cards!
